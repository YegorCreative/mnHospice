Who We Are

Care That Feels
Like Home.

Minnesota Hospice Care is a nonprofit organization born from Minnesota’s deep tradition of looking after one another. Since 1994, we’ve walked alongside patients and families during life’s most sacred passage — bringing the same honest, neighbor-first care that defines this state.

Our interdisciplinary teams serve patients in their homes, nursing facilities, and assisted living communities across the Twin Cities metro and greater Minnesota — wherever home is.

Our Foundation

Rooted in
Minnesota Values

The land shapes the people. Our care reflects the best of what it means to call Minnesota home.

Quiet Strength

The north woods don’t shout. Neither do we. Our care speaks through unwavering, steady presence — through every season of the journey.

Still Waters

Like Minnesota’s 10,000 lakes, we bring calm to the hardest moments. Our presence is a peaceful anchor for families in the storm.

Neighbor Care

Before care systems, there were neighbors. We carry that tradition — the genuine, roll-up-your-sleeves Minnesota way of showing up when it matters most.

Four Seasons

Through spring’s hope, summer’s fullness, autumn’s beauty, and winter’s peace — we walk with every family through every season of the final journey.

What We Provide

Comprehensive Hospice Services

Our care extends far beyond medicine — we support the mind, body, and spirit of every person we serve.

Personal Care

Skilled nursing, aide support, and medical management tailored to each patient's unique needs.

24/7 Availability

Around-the-clock nurse access and on-call physicians ready to respond any time, day or night.

In-Home Support

Care delivered where patients are most comfortable — at home, in a facility, or wherever they call home.

Spiritual Care

Chaplains and counselors provide comfort, meaning, and peace regardless of faith background.

Bereavement Support

Grief counseling and follow-up care for families for up to 13 months after a loved one passes.

Volunteer Program

Trained volunteers offer companionship, caregiver respite, and community connection to patients.
